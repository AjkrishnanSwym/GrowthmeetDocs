# SMB Retention · H2 2026

*Part of SMB Conversion + Retention. This document covers the Retention half; Conversion is covered separately.*

Retention is the quiet half of the SMB number, and the cheapest growth we have. Wishlist Plus is the cash engine that funds everything, so every SMB merchant we keep is recurring revenue that compounds at no reacquisition cost. This proposal covers the retention half of SMB Conversion + Retention. The H2 goal is to reduce ICP churn by roughly **$500 to $1,000 MRR**, and to do it AI-first, so the cost of retaining does not scale with the number of merchants we retain.

## The opportunity, and the number

SMB churn concentrates early. Merchants who never get fully live, or who cannot see and feel the value they are getting, leave in the first weeks, and often on price. That is also where churn is most preventable, because the causes are ours to fix. Reducing churn by **$500 to $1,000 MRR** in H2 protects the base that funds the rest of the plan, and it is a number we move by fixing causes, not by discounting.

## AI-first, applied to retention

We run retention the way the offsite defines AI-first: **AI by default, human by exception.** Support, health-monitoring, and value reporting are high-frequency and repeatable, so they belong to AI first. A person steps in only where getting it wrong would damage a merchant relationship, which is the second question of the test. And we build before we hire: as retained volume grows, the first move is to automate, not to add headcount, and we still own the outcome of every agent we put in the loop.

## The retention engine, BAU made AI-first

Three things have to be true for an SMB merchant to stay. Each is run AI-first, and the first is the one that matters most.

- **Value is maximized, and the merchant sees it.** This is the deepest retention lever: a merchant whose business Swym is measurably growing does not leave, and does not churn on price. We grow Swym-attributed revenue as a rising share of merchant GMV, deepen adoption across touchpoints, and surface that value back to the merchant through AI-driven reporting. Keeping a merchant and growing them are the same motion.
- **Setup is done and working on the storefront.** Merchants who never get live never see value, and churn early. We detect incomplete or broken storefront setup automatically, fix and nudge without a human in the standard path, and reserve human effort for high-value merchants.
- **The product works as expected,** so support is never a reason to leave. AI-first deflection and Fin resolution handle the repeatable contacts at low cost; humans take the long tail and remove repeat-contact drivers at the source.

## H2 initiatives

Three sequential initiatives make the number, each an AI-first move I own. They are ordered by leverage, with value maximization first.

1. **Maximize and prove value.** Grow Swym-attributed revenue as a share of merchant GMV, deepen adoption across touchpoints, and make that value visible to the merchant through AI-driven reporting. This is the primary retention lever: merchants who can see Swym growing their business stay, and expand. Measured by Swym share of GMV and adoption depth.
2. **Storefront activation assurance.** Detect automatically when a merchant's Swym setup is not live or not rendering on their storefront, nudge and fix without a human in the standard path, and reserve human effort for high-value accounts. This gets merchants to value in the first place, and attacks early churn at its most common cause.
3. **Churn-risk early warning and save.** AI watches usage and health signals, flags at-risk merchants before they leave, and triggers save flows; AI-first deflection and Fin resolution keep support from ever being a reason to leave, and humans own the high-value saves.

## What is true by year end

Two things. SMB churn is measurably lower, down by **$500 to $1,000 MRR**, and the motion that got us there is AI by default, so it holds as we grow rather than needing more people to sustain it. Above all, retention is driven by maximized, visible value, which is what turns keeping a merchant and growing them into a single motion.
