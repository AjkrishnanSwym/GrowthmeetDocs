# Retention & Expansion: growth of the base (offsite overview)



## The one thesis

Growth of the 8,000- paid store base = **retention** (keep the MRR we have) + **expansion** (grow MRR per merchant). Both are the cheapest, highest-leverage growth we have, and both are outputs of the same motion: move the Intent Conversion Score, prove it, and wire into the merchant's stack. **We do not need a new investment bucket to grow the base.**

Why now, from the June 2026 analysis:
- Monthly new (~$8.5k) ≈ monthly churn (~$9.3k). The SMB base is on a treadmill, so **every point of churn removed is worth as much as a point of new acquisition.**
- Net growth today comes almost entirely from **expansion, overwhelmingly Enterprise** (~12% of subs, ~45% of MRR).
- Trial-to-paid is already healthy (~75%). The block is reaching the right merchants and keeping the ones we win, not converting them.

---

## Retention (the quiet half, the cheapest growth)

Wishlist Plus is the cash engine that funds everything, so every SMB merchant we keep compounds at no reacquisition cost. **H2 goal: reduce SMB churn by ~$500 to $1,000 MRR**, done **AI-first (AI by default, human by exception)** so the cost of retaining does not scale with merchants retained.

Three things must be true for a merchant to stay, in order of leverage:

1. **Value is maximized and the merchant sees it.** The deepest lever: grow Swym-attributed revenue as a share of GMV, deepen adoption, and surface that value back through AI-driven reporting. Keeping and growing a merchant are the same motion.
2. **Setup is live and working on the storefront.** Detect broken/incomplete setup automatically, fix and nudge without a human in the standard path, reserve people for high-value accounts.
3. **The product works, so support is never a reason to leave.** AI-first deflection and Fin handle the repeatable contacts; humans take the long tail.

---

## Expansion (where net growth already comes from)

The same three H2 buckets each produce expansion as a byproduct of moving the score:

- **Value for merchants** - the missed-value signal is the honest cross-sell engine: every leak points to the next surface and the next plan tier.
- **AI-first** - the agent continuously surfaces the next opportunity, turning expansion from a campaign into an always-on loop (the ">3-trigger merchants" lever).
- **Integrations & partnerships** - integration depth is switching cost (retention) and the runway for Enterprise expansion, the growth engine in the forecast.

**The near-term win: an upgrade reach-out to non-basic, high-session, still-unpaid merchants.** Merchants on Plus/professional/unlimited with real traffic and intent activity but no paid Swym plan are telling us, in their own data, that they are ready to pay. Product supplies the missed-value-per-store signal; **PMM runs the reach-out** (in-app, lifecycle, human touch for the largest).

## Cross-dependencies (needed from outside the buckets)

| Dependency | Why it matters | Owner |

| **Agency motions** | Distribute the score across whole portfolios: retention at scale and the advisory channel for plan uplift and Enterprise expansion. | Partnerships |

| **Analytics / Beacon depth** | The measurement floor retention proof and expansion targeting both stand on. | Analytics |

---

## What is true by year end

- **SMB churn is measurably lower** (down ~$500 to $1,000 MRR), and the motion that got us there is **AI by default**, so it holds as we grow.
- **Expansion runs on the missed-value signal**, with the non-basic-unpaid upgrade motion converting latent value into paid MRR.
- Above all, retention is driven by **maximized, visible value**, which is what makes keeping a merchant and growing them a single motion.
